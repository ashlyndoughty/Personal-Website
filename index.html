Athletic Body: A Study

"Choose a performative photographer by researching online. Be inspired! Choose a photograph that interests you and recreate the image. Focus on artistic style - what is the message, and does it affect the way the audience views your piece?" - VPSB75 Photo II, artistic direction.

I chose Aaron Siskind's "Pleasures and Terrors of Levitation" series. He captured the body in beautiful montions that emphasised human form without hypersexualization of the models. I chose a model with many physical attributes to participate in my recreation.

  • When: September 2014
  • What: Independent Artist Study

Sir Robert Borden, The Man Behind The Politics

"We will be working alongside Paris Roger to create a functioning website that focuses on Sir Robert Borden’s personal life rather than his political endeavours. The purpose of this site is to create a website that users can access to learn about Borden in an interactive manner, as well as encourage users to watch Paris Roger’s documentary 'Sir Robert Borden: The Man Behind the Politics'." - Scope Statement & Project Definition

As a team, we drew up wireframes and prototypes for the website's design. After some revisions, we got approval from the client to create a parallax scrolling site that included: a film synopsis, a video trailer, an image gallery, historical information, and methods of contact. Individually, I was responsible for the historical research and photo collection.

In addition, we created a 70 page final report documenting the entire process. From scope statements to moodboard and wireframes, research to prototypes, revisions to the final design.

  • When: January 2013 - April 2013

Choking Hazard

"Illustrate a dream or nightmare using an expressionist photography style. A person must be present and interacting with an object." - VPSC58 Photo III, artistic direction

Vast amounts of trash are produced everyday. If it isn't littered on sidewalks and parks, it's being piled into the landfills, accumulating tonnes of waste in every city, every country, every year. Plastic is a large component in today's manufacturing, and has become very problematic as it isn't biodegradable and is hurting our ecosystems. The plastic bag acts as a gag, keeping her submissive and unable to break free from society's great need for consummerism.

This work was displayed in the UofT Student Exhibition, 2015.

  • Who: University of Toronto - VPSC58 Photo III
  • When: February 2015
  • What: Expressionist photography

I Am Canadian

"Visual communication relies both on eyes that function and on a brain that interprets all sensory information received. An active, curious mind remembers and uses visual messages in thoughtful and innovative ways. Knowing about the world and the images that it conveys will help you analyze pictures. And if you can examine pictures critically, you have a good chance of producing high-quality images that others will remember." - Paul Martin Lester, Visual Communication: Images with Messages

I researched different aspects of Canadian culture that revolved around hockey. Every posting relates back to at least one of the six perspectives: cultural, critical, technical, historical, ethical, and/or personal. Maintained a personal blog feel while keeping it professional at the same time.

  • Who: University of Toronto - MDSB64 Visual Culture
  • When: February 2012 - April 2012
  • What: Independent Visual Analysis Project
  • Where: withglowing-hearts
